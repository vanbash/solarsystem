What is Solar System?

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the object that orbit it either directly or indirectly.


Formation

The Solar System formed 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.


Star

The Sun is the star at the center of the Solar System. It is a nearly perfect sphere of hot plasma, with internal convective motion that generates a magnetic field via a dynamo process. It is by far the most important source of energy for life on Earth.


Planets

A planet is an astronomical body orbiting a star or stellar remnant that is massive enough to be rounded by its own gravity, is not massive enough to cause thermonuclear fusion, and has cleared its neighbouring region of planetesimals. In Solar System are eight planets. We can divide them into inner, which are smaller and outer, which are larger.

Inner planets:

  1. Mercury
  2. Venus
  3. Earth
  4. Mars

They are primarily composed of rock and metal. Earth is the only astronomical object known to harbor life.

Outer planets:

  1. Jupiter
  2. Saturn
  3. Uranus
  4. Neptune

The two largest, Jupiter and Saturn, are gas giants, being composed mainly of hydrogen and helium; the two outermost planets, Uranus and Neptune, are ice giants.
